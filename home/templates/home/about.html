About Megadodo Publications

"The publishing company that refused to go quietly into the night, or indeed any other time of day."

Our Story

Megadodo Publications was founded several million years ago in the Ursa Minor Beta system by a group of researchers who had grown tired of not knowing things. Armed with towels, a worrying amount of optimism and a secondhand spacecraft, they set out to compile the definitive guide to absolutely everything.

The result was The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy — the standard repository of all knowledge and wisdom, and the best-selling book in the history of the known universe. It outsold the Encyclopedia Galactica, largely because it was slightly cheaper and had the words DON'T PANIC printed in large friendly letters on the cover.

Since then, Megadodo Publications has expanded its catalogue to include works on zero gravity activities, celestial homecare, theological philosophy and the correct way to carry a towel. We are proud of every single one of them, including the ones that got us banned from four solar systems.

Our Mission

To publish books that are, on the whole, more useful than not having them. We believe that knowledge should be accessible to everyone, regardless of species, planet of origin or current relationship with the laws of physics.

Our Team

Megadodo Publications employs researchers across the galaxy who are dedicated to finding things out, writing them down and occasionally surviving the experience. Our editorial team reviews all submissions carefully before publication, except on Fridays.

A Note on Accuracy

We make every effort to ensure the information in our publications is accurate at the time of going to press. However, given the size of the universe and the speed at which things change, some entries may be slightly out of date. The entry on Earth, for example, was last updated shortly before it was demolished. We apologise for any inconvenience.

Don't Panic. We know what we're doing. Mostly.
